What’s different on load — three moments, one message

Toggle Current / Proposed above to compare. Everything a returning guest already knows is exactly where they left it.

  1. 1
    “Book VIP” tab in the header

    The only filled-gold tab in the nav, with a NEW pip — a permanent destination that survives after the ribbon is dismissed. Sits beside The Guide so booking reads as a peer to asking.

  2. 2
    Announcement ribbon under the header

    The one truly new stripe of chrome on load. A single gold sheen plays twice, then it sits quietly; it’s dismissible, and once dismissed it stays gone — the tab and the card carry on.

  3. 3
    VIP direct-booking card in The Guide’s welcome

    Inserted between the welcome copy and the familiar prompt chips. States the offer plainly — direct rates, upgrade, breakfast, credit — with a primary “book it yourself” path and a secondary path that keeps The Guide in the loop.

Unchanged: the welcome question and copy, all five starter chips, the composer, the draggable gutter, the Living Atlas pane and its controls, and every existing nav tab. The globe here is a static stand-in for the live Mapbox canvas.
